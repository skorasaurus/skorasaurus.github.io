The age of Cleveland Buildings

Some observations:

During the 1930s, the rate of new building construction plummeted and left several streets like West 158th St. to have adjacent houses built 10-15 years apart from each other.

Those unfamiliar with the East side will be surprised to see the small pockets of market-rate housing during the 1990s at East 36th-38th between Central and Community College and East 82nd-87th, between Chester and Euclid

Also, there's some of my favorite houses in Cleveland, a small block of some dense housing right in Downtown, on 13th and Lindazzo. They were built in 07 and with the housing market crashing, any other pending construction there was postponed...

The raw data for further analysis is available at http://github.com/skorasaurus/clebuildings
